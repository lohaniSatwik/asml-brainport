Part 2 — Societal Value

World Class Society: the key findings

Economic success does not automatically translate into societal wellbeing. These findings show both the achievements — and the tensions.

11%
Brainport contributes ~11% of Dutch GDP
One small region in southern Netherlands drives a disproportionate share of the entire national economy — through a tightly connected cluster of tech companies, universities, and suppliers.
4/day
Four patents filed every single day
Brainport companies file an average of 4 patents per day. This continuous innovation feeds the education system: TU/e and Fontys are deeply embedded in the cluster, raising the long-term knowledge level of the region.
↑↑
But housing costs and inequality are rising
High-earning expats and engineers compete with locals for housing. Many residents feel the benefits of the tech boom are out of reach. "World Class Industry" and "World Class Society" don't happen automatically together — that gap is the core tension.
Part 3 — European Values

How Brainport ties into EU values

The EU Chips Act committed €43 billion to boost European semiconductor capacity. Brainport is its flagship — and it embodies core European principles.

🛡️
Strategic Autonomy
80%+ of world's chips made with ASML machines. Europe cannot afford to lose this technology to geopolitical risk.
🌱
Sustainability
The cluster is investing in green chip manufacturing — lower water use, lower energy intensity — aligned with the EU Green Deal.
🤝
Social Cohesion
European values demand that prosperity is shared. Inclusive growth — not just for engineers — is the societal challenge for Brainport.
🌐
Open Partnerships
ASML thrives on international talent and global supply chains — proving that openness and security can and must coexist.
"Brainport is not just an economic asset — it is Europe's most important proof of concept. The question is not whether it creates value. The question is whether that value is shared."
